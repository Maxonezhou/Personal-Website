Introduction

One of the largest threats to firefighters is the inability to effectively monitor several different environmental vitals at the same time. Additional equipment and communication devices used by firefighters increase bulk and impede their ability to move when stuck in tough situations. Our team sought to tackle this issue by developing a novel, lightweight solution for firefighters keep track of important vitals and provide a central location to monitor the health of all the firefighters at the same time. Built for Hack the North 2019, Guardian is a novel helmet designed to advance current firefighting methods. By attaching various crucial sensors on the helmet, we are able to gather important vitals about the environment such as temperature, CO2 levels, atmospheric pressure, volume of total organic compounds, and much more while simultaneously sending this data in real-time over the Internet of Things platform to a remote database. We then retrieved this information and created a unique web user interface that responded to the real-time changes in data to relay information across all individuals over watching the situation.
